Why is Exercise Important?

This is a common question that many people ask about fitness.
"Why should I exercise?"
"It's just a waste of time"
Here is a list of eight ways that exercising regularly can better anyone's life.


  1. It can help with relaxation and sleep quality

    2. When people exercise and feel tired afterwards, they enjoy their relaxation more and can often fall asleep more easily.




  2. Exercise can help with brain health and memory

    3. Studies show that exercises both give you a mental break and increase bloodflow to your brain.
    This in turn improves cognitive function and memory skills.


  3. Exercise can help skin health

    4. Recent studies show that moderate excercise can actually improve skin health.
    It has even recently been shown that regular excercise can slow skin aging and wrinkles.

  4. Exercise can reduce risk of chronic disease

    5. Exercise has been shown to reduce the risk of:

    • Type 2 diabeties
    • Heart disease
    • Cancer
    • High cholesterol
    • Hypertension

  5. Exercise can increase your energy levels

    6. Contrary to popular belief, exercise can actually improve your energy levels!
    This is because a better cardiovascular system and better lung health allow your body to use less energy while performing everyday tasks.


  6. Exercise is good for your bones and muscles

    7. Exercise breaks down your muscles and thickens your bones.
    This causes muscles to come back stronger.
    Thicker, stronger muscles and bones will last better with age. General streangth is also just helpful to have through life.

  7. Exercise can help with weight management

    8. No matter your weight goals, a healthy diet paired with the appropriate exercise can help you reach them!
    If you want to lose weight, pair a calorie deficit with cardio exercises.
    If you want to gain weight, the best way to do so is by pairing weight training with a calorie surplus.

  8. Exercise makes you feel happier

    9. There are few better feelings as a human than feeling the accomplishment after compling a hard workout.
    On top of that, being in better shape will make life easier, in turn making it easier to be happy.
    Studies have shown that regular exercise increases dopamine levels in the brain and helps to cure depression, slowly but surely.
